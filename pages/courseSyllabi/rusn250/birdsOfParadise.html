Sirin and Alkonost. Birds of Sorrow and Joy. An oil painting (1896), artist Viktor Vasnetsov The birds came to Slavic mythology from Greek myths about sirens. In Greek mythology sirens are humanlike beings with alluring voices. For example, they appear in Homer's Odyssey and almost kill Odissey and his crew.

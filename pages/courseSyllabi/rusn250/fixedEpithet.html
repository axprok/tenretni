Fixed Epithet

Fixed Epithet: The repeated use of a word or phrase for the same person, place, or object. ⇒ The noun always goes along with the same (fixed) adjective. The fixed epithet is a literary device commonly used by Homer in the The Iliad and The Odyssey (“gray-eyed Athena,” “ox-eyed Hera,” "fleet-footed Achilles," and “wine-red Sea”)

    Examples in Russian folk songs and literature include:
  • little birch
  • thick berries
  • green meadows
  • warm summer
  • fair maiden
  • brave youth
  • red Sun
  • holy Russia

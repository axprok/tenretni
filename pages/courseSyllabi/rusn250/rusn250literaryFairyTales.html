Literary Fairy Tales

Alexander Pushkin. Tale of Tsar Saltan" (1831)
based on the plot of a folk take "Wicked Sisters". Written in trochaic tetrameter, a meter imitating the meter of the folk song. A common folktale in Belarus, Russia, and Ulraine. You can read more about Pushkin's uses of folklore in Michael Wachtel's article "Pushkin's Turn to Folklore."

    Art Works inspired by the "Tale of Tsar Saltan"
  • Ivan Bilibin's illustrations (1905)
  • Nikolai Rimskii-Korsakov opera "Tale of Tsar Saltan" (1900). We listened to a small interlude from the opera "The Flight of a Bumble-Bee." The piece closes Act III, Tableau 1, during which the magic Swan-Bird changes Prince Gvidon Saltanovich (the Tsar's son) into an insect so that he can fly away to visit his father

Tatyana Tolstaya. "Date with a Bird" (1983)
Tolstaya uses folkloric image of a bird of paradise Sirin. Excellent use of metaphor.

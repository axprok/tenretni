The Modern Fantastic

According to Tsvetan Todorov, the modern fantastic is that hesitation experienced by a person who knows only the laws of nature, confronting an apparently supernatural event. Only in the hesitation between deciding which of those two applies can the fantastic be found. (Todorov, Tzvetan, The Fantastic: A Structural Approach to a Literary Genre 1973)

    Examples
  • Alexander Pushkin. "Queen of Spades" (1831)
  • Nikolai Gogol. "Nose"
  • Nikolai Gogol. "Night Before Christmas" (1832)

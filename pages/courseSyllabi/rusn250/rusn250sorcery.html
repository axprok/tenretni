Russian Folk Belief: Sorcery and Healing.

Linda Ivanits argues that Russian sorcery is different from the Western tradition. Witchcraft and sorcery in Russia distinctly lacks the demonology inherent in Western witchcraft. Instead Russian peasants believed in panteistic (nature spirits) sources of supernatural.

Double faith. Ivanits also argues that folk sorcery refers to belief in a host of supernatural powerful beings, not sanctioned by dominant religion (orthodox Christianity in this case), but persisting side by side with the official religion.

Socialist Realism and Fairy Tales

Katerina Clark on socialist realist master plot
Socialist realism is the official method of Soviet culture (1934-1991).
The key elements of socialist realist narrative are
positive hero,
mentor,
the reeducation plot.

Examples of the socialist realist master plot Kazanskii's film Old Genie Khottabych (1956): communist scout Volka re-educates his magic helper.

Sergei Eisenstein's Alexander Nevskii (1938): the epic prince Alexander re-educates people of Novgorod.

Marfa Kriukova's "Tale of the Pole" (1937): the novina (a pseudo-folk epic) about Stalin's polar pilots and explorers who tame (re-educate) polar bears

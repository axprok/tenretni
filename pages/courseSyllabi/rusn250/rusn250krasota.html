Krasota or Volia

This is a maiden's headdress called Krasota. It is open in the back so that the girl's braid would be visible, and it was worn by Russian girls before they got married. Part of the wedding ritual was the removal of this headdress in preparation for the married woman's headdress, which usually covered all of the woman's hair. During this ritual the bride sang songs about her Krasota and how she did not want to part with it, but by the end of the ritual the bride usually passed her Krasota on to a younger relative - a sister, sister-in-law, or niece.

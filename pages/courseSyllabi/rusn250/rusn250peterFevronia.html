The Tale of Peter and Fevronia of Murom
author: Hermolaus-Erasmus, a priest in one of Kremlin churces (1500s (16th century))

    Folk tale motifs in The Tale of Peter and Fevronia of Murom:
  • a prince's victory over a snake or dragon
  • a prince's magical healing by a wise maiden
  • the motif of a wise woman outwitting lustful men and protecting her honor
    Hagiographic motifs The Tale of Peter and Fevronia of Murom:
  • Miracles after saints' death: Peter and Fevronia are buried separately but the next day end up in the same burial site

In Russia, the Day of Saints Peter and Fevronia (July 8) is celebrated as the Day of Love and Fidelity. They are considered as the patrons of marriage and family.

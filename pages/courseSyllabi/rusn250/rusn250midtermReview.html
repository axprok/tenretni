    Approaches to Folklore and Folktales
  • Jakobson and Bogatyrev’s theory about folklore and its difference from literature (langue and parole, innovation, authorship)
  • thematic (animal, household, wonder tales) and motif approaches to the folk tale
  • Propp’s structuralist approach and how to apply it to tales
  • Luthi’s stylistic approach and how to apply it to tales (Abstract Style, One-Dimensionality, Depthlessness)
  • Bettelheim’s theory, how he categorizes different tales (and features of those types of tales) (“Youngest Child,” “Two Sibling,”)
  • Feminist approach to the folktale: Marcia Lieberman’s reading of folk tale as a genre
    Characteristics of different types of tales from this unit: “Bad Wives,” “Wise Maidens,” “Wicked Stepmothers,” “Snow White,” “Sleeping Beauty”

(She's cannibalistic!) She is sometimes like a fairy godmother, but sometimes like a wicked witch, and therefore she is neither. -She (like Koshchey) is intimately connected to and has power over nature. -She has all sorts of objects associated with her: a house on chicken legs, her mortar, pestle, and broom, -She is lanky, long-nosed, and has a bony leg. She also has a great sense of smell and often “smells Russians”. -She often appears with siblings or daughters. -She is an asexual type of character and has both “masculine” and “feminine” traits. -She does not want to air out her dirty laundry (figuratively speaking...) Koshchei the Deathless Some facts about Koshchei (sometimes spelled Koshchey) the deathless, a character you will meet in this week's stories: -In Russian, he is Koshchei Bessmertnyi (Literally “Koshchei without death”) -He is perpetually aged yet ageless. He is both immortal and mortal, old and young. -He is skinny and bony with scraggly hair, and therefore suspicious. -He is intimately connected with nature: his death is hidden away and protected by representatives of many realms of the natural world; he seems to have some power over natural forces. -Traditionally, the fairy tale motif usually associated with him is that the tale’s hero must find Koshchei’s death in order to defeat him. -He can be a father figure. -He can be outsmarted. -He is a representative of the importance of secrecy.

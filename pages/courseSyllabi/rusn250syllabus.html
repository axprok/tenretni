I. Course Overview Course Description This course comprises four categories of texts, both verbal and visual: (1) a survey of Russian folk belief, which illustrates the animistic nature of Russian popular beliefs about the world that persist to this day; (2) a large selection of the best-known Russian folk tales, to be compared with German and English tales, epics; (3) scholarly articles analyzing the differences between folklore and literature and representing various theoretical/critical schools: Structuralism, Marxism, Freudianism; and feminism; (4) literary fantastic tales. Visual materials (films, paintings, graphics, and handicrafts) and music inspired by Russian folklore and fairy tales figure regularly in the course.

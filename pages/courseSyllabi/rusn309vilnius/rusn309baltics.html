Rusn 309: Lithuania and Russia in Context

William & Mary/European Humanities University

    The course offers students a broad perspective on Russia and Baltic nations through Russian, Lithuanian, and Eastern European cinema. The specific focus will be on Lithuania. Through assigned readings, film screenings, guest talks, and excursions students will examine
  • the memory of anti-colonial resistance by Lithuanians
  • history and long-lasting effects of Soviet and Russian imperial domination of the region
  • the geography and brief history of the Russia and the USSR the
  • the memory of World War II on the land and the peoples of this territory

Students undertake an independent research project on an aspect of the long history of interactions between Lithuania and surrounding nations and empires. By the end of the course, students will not only have a strong grounding in both Russian and Lithuanian history, but also become aware of the interconnected nature of these countriesâ€™ histories.

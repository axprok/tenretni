Rusn 309: Lithuania and Russia in Context

William & Mary/European Humanities University

    The course offers students a broad perspective on Russia and Baltic nations
    through Russian, Lithuanian, and Eastern European cinema.
    The specific focus will be on Lithuania.
    Through assigned readings, film screenings, guest talks, and excursions students will examine
  • the memory of anti-colonial resistance by Lithuanians
  • history and long-lasting effects of Soviet and Russian imperial domination of the region
  • the geography and brief history of the Russia and the USSR the
  • the memory of World War II on the land and the peoples of this territory

Methodology
The present course, a continuation of the discussions begun in the separate one-credit course in the spring 2025 semester, is designed to help you experience Lithuania as it has interacted in its often difficult history with its at times hostile neighbor, Russia. These complex interactions between the histories of Lithuania and Russia will be guided by the following question: “Who Wants Whom to Remember What, Why, When, and Where?” It is thus animated by a notion of the constructed and contested nature of history and the past.

While political borders conveniently define the boundaries of contemporary Russia, the area variously referred to as post-Soviet, Eastern European, and/or Eurasian has more nebulous contours. This is a vast space that stretches across eleven time zones and is home to over 100 different ethnic groups. What do we learn by studying it as a unitary region despite its multicultural complexity and political diversity?

Using approaches from disciplines such as history, political science, anthropology, film studies, literary studies, and journalism, this course explores the scope and meaning of Russia, Eastern Europe, and Eurasia as a region of study. We will see the strong links that bind the different parts of this region not only historically and linguistically but also geopolitically. By the end of the course, you will have a deeper understanding of the ways in which the legacy of the socialist past as well as common socio-political, economic, and cultural traits in the present define this region and distinguish it from other areas of the world.

The course will be taught in four thematically and disciplinary focused units: Soviet history; post-Soviet developments; Central Asia and the Caucasus; and environment and health. The course will also include the vision of films and the regular reading and discussion of news pertaining to the region. Through these activities, you will develop an understanding of current salient issues and be sufficiently prepared for deeper, specialized study of the region in the social sciences and humanities. The course will include sections on methodology and how to identify reliable source material. Finally, while working on the completion of your final projects, you will improve your analytical and writing skills.

REES 201 serves as a gateway course for students interested in majoring in Russian and East European Studies, but it also welcomes students who simply want to learn more about this region from an interdisciplinary perspective. Please note that this class carries the COLL 200 attribute.

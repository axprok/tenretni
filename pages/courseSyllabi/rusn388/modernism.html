Modernism

a definition from Routledge Encyclopedia of Modernism: "Russian Modernism (roughly 1890s–1930s), known as the "Silver Age," was a profound aesthetic, philosophical, and social departure from 19th-century realism. It prioritized subjectivity, mysticism, formal experimentation, and the synthesis of arts (poetry, music, painting). It evolved from Symbolism and Decadence into a radical Avant-Garde (Futurism, Acmeism) before being suppressed by Socialist Realism"

Modernism in design and architecture emerged in the aftermath of the First World War and the Russian Revolution – a period when the artistic avant-garde dreamed of a new world free from history and tradition.

Modernist writers experimented with literary form and expression, as exemplified by Ezra Pound's maxim to "Make it new."

Vladimir Mayakovsky (1893-1930)

Mayakovsky revolutionized poetic language and rebeled against the status quo.
A devoted socialist, he joined the revolutionary Bolshevik Party in 1908. For several months, Mayakovsky and his comrades opposed the Tsarist regime in the streets of Moscow.
For instance, they organized the biggest escape from a female prison in Russia’s history.
In Mayakovsky’s case, police caught the young revolutionary and put him behind bars for 11 months.

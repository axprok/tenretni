Vladimir Mayakovsky. And Could You?

An actor reads the English translation of the poem.

Comparing Mayakovsky tonic poem with
what if he wrote his poem in polite syllabo-tonic verse.

Bad syllabo-tonic English version
(Regular iambic pentameter, smooth, literary, dead on arrival)
I swiftly blurred the map of daily life
With paint that splashed out brightly from a glass.
Upon a silver dish of jellyed meat
I showed the ocean’s cheekbones, sharply drawn.

And on the scales of some metallic fish
I read the tender calling of new lips.
Now tell me, could you softly play a tune,
A nocturne on a pipe of flowing drains?

What’s wrong here
Predictable da-DUM da-DUM da-DUM
Verbs softened (blurred, showed, play a tune)
Syntax smoothed into literary English
The challenge turns into a gentle invitation
This poem could be read sitting down. That’s for losers and boring middle class goody-goody kids.


TONIC version (Stress-driven, jagged, performative, intentionally uneven)

Could you?

I-at once—smeared the map of the everyday,
splashing paint from a glass.
I showed--on a dish of aspic--the slanted cheekbones of the ocean.
On the scales of a tin fish
I read the calls of new lips.
And you—
could you
play
a nocturne
on a flute
of drainpipes?

Day of the Oprichnik. Sorokin's immitation of a medieval folk epic

Here Sorokin shifts registers quite noticeably. This passage imitates the style of Russian epic folklore—specifically the bylina (былина) tradition. Key features that signal this: Formulaic repetition and parallelism: “А у каждой главы… / А перву главу… / А другу главу…” — this incremental, patterned buildup is characteristic of oral epic performance, where repetition aids memory and creates rhythm. Archaic numeration and forms: “перву,” “другу,” “третью-то” — these are stylized, pseudo-archaic grammatical forms typical of how byliny are edited or performed. Epic naming formulas (“зовут-кличут”): Doubling verbs like “зовут-кличут” is a classic folkloric device, reinforcing oral cadence and ceremonial naming. Mythological subject matter: The multi-headed serpent explicitly invokes Zmey Gorynych, a central antagonist in East Slavic epic tales. Crescendo toward a collective identity: The final reveal—“всех-то нас семиглавых-то… Змеем Горынычем”—mirrors the epic technique of delaying the full identification of a powerful being.

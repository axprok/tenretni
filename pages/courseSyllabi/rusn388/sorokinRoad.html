Day of the Oprichnik: the Road

Sorokin turns the Road into a dystopian image of globalization under neo-feudal conditions: technologically modern but politically archaic.

In Day of the Oprichnik, “the Road” is not just a highway but a major symbolic and geopolitical structure. Sorokin imagines a future Russia dominated by a new autocracy and economically tied to China through the trans-Eurasian transportation corridor.

The Road embodies several things at once:
imperial power and centralized control
Russia’s dependence on Chinese commerce and technology
the fusion of medieval brutality with futuristic infrastructure

In Day of the Oprichnik, the clairvoyant — Praskovia Mamontovna — lives near Orenburg. Andrei Komiaga travels there to consult her on behalf of the Tsarina. The journey to Orenburg also emphasizes the novel’s eastward orientation toward China and the Eurasian frontier in Vladimir Sorokin’s dystopian Russia.

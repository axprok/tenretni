Course Goals

  • Trace the development of twentieth-century and twenty-first century Russophone literatures within the context of the profound cultural, social, and political transformations of the Russo-Soviet period.
  • Examine the role of literature in Russian social life and political discourse.
  • Analyze representative literary works alongside the cultural and ideological debates that shaped their production and reception, with particular attention to constructions of the “new hero” in modernist, socialist realist, and postmodernist writing.
  • Introduce students to cultural criticism by exploring how contemporary critics interpreted and evaluated the artistic output of their time.
  • Familiarize students with the major literary movements in twentieth-century and twenty-first-century Russian poetry and prose.
  • Explore parallel developments in cinema and discuss the dynamic interactions between literary and filmic forms.

Day of the Oprichnik (2006) satirizes nationalist nostalgia for a mythic past

    Examples
  • A pious religious sermon by Batya (Boris, the head of oprichniks) who has just ordered to kill Count Urusov several pages before. The sermon is happening under the influence of coke and vodka: "Batya crosses himself. We cross ouserlves as well. We snort some more" (179-181)
  • Investigation of the subversive poem about a maniac who has sex with victims of fire during fires. The criminal ends up being the member of tsar's family and the oprichniks decide not to go public about both crimes and the poem

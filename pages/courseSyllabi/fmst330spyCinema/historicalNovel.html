Historical Novel

The historical novel is a significant precursor to the spy novel. Both genres emerged within the context of British imperial culture.

    Major Practitioners
  • Sir Walter Scott (British Empire) Waverley (1816)
  • James Fenimore Cooper (US) The Last of the Mohicans (1826)
  • Alexander Pushkin (Russia) Captain's Daughter (1836)
    Devices
  • the lead character is fictional caught between two camps
  • historical characters and events provide framework but fictional characters carry the main plot. Real historical figures appear, yet remain secondary.
  • both sides of a historical conflict sympathetically, avoiding simple moral binaries. Even defeated causes are treated with dignity.
  • Attention to Local Color and Detail: Dialects and speech patterns (native American names Hawkeye), customs, dress, clegal and religious practices, technology of the time

technology of the time in The Last of the Mohicans. The Pennsylvania rifle (often called the Kentucky rifle) is a long, rifled firearm developed in colonial America in the early 18th century. The rifle was created by German gunsmiths who settled in Pennsylvania, especially in the Lancaster region. The rifle became a symbol of the American frontier and individual marksmanship, closely associated with figures like Hawkeye.

The Pennsylvania (Kentucky) rifle becomes a powerful symbolic object in early American historical fiction—especially in the novels of James Fenimore Cooper. In The Last of the Mohicans Cooper’s frontier hero Hawkeye is inseparable from his long rifle, “Killdeer.”
The rifle represents f rontier skill and individual mastery. The rifle symbolizes precision, patience, and self-reliance—qualities that distinguish the American frontiersman from European soldiers.
Democratic heroism. Unlike aristocratic swords (symbols of nobility), the rifle is a practical tool. It suggests a new kind of hero rooted in skill rather than lineage.
Harmony with nature. Hawkeye uses the rifle primarily for hunting and survival. It reflects his deep knowledge of the wilderness rather than blind violence.
Cultural mediatio. Like Scott’s mediating hero, Hawkeye stands between Native American and European worlds. His rifle, European in origin but transformed on American soil, mirrors this hybridity.

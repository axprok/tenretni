James Fenimore Cooper (1789-1851)

James Fenimore Cooper is best known for his Leatherstocking Tales, especially The Last of the Mohicans, a series of frontier novels featuring the iconic scout Natty Bumppo (for the midterm his name is Nathaniel Poe (aka Hawkeye), which helped create the American Western genre and shape national identity by romanticizing the wilderness. The Last of the Mohicans was the first American novel to become popular outside of the US.

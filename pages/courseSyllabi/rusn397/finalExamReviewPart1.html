rusn 397 Final Exam Study Guide. Part I.

    Part I: Dostoevsky’s Major Works
  • 1. Name Symbolism
    • Functions of first names, diminutives, full names (e.g., Rodion / Rodya / Rodion Romanovich Raskolnikov).
    • How name “shifts” mirror a character’s psychological condition or social role.
    • Examples from Crime and Punishment and The Brothers Karamazov.
  • 2. Divine Justice vs. Secular Law
    • Comparison of magistrates, legal officers, and judges in the two novels.
    • How Dostoevsky stages conflict between religious morality and legal justice.
    • Raskolnikov and Dmitri: Their relationships to investigators. Their perception of moral vs. legal guilt.
  • 3. Doubling in The Brothers Karamazov
    • Father–son patterns; generational conflict.
    • Ideological doubles (Ivan/Alyosha; Ivan/Smerdyakov; Dmitri/Fyodor).
    • Doubling as moral testing.
  • 4. Underground Man Characteristics
    • Philosophy (irrationalism, spite, hyper-consciousness).
    • Narrative stance (unreliable,
    • self-contradictory, aggressive intimacy).
    • Physical/living conditions; isolation; illness as symbol.
    • Male and female doubles of the Underground Man in The Brothers Karamazov (e.g., Ivan, Lise, sometimes Smerdyakov or Katerina Ivanovna).
  • 5. Scandal Scene characteristics.
  • 6. Creative Prompt: Crime and Punishment in America
    • “Most premeditated and abstract” American city: choose and justify.
    • Apartment description; new transgressive theory.
    • The savior figure: gender, occupation, background, where they live, how they meet.
    • The double(s): who they are; who insists on similarity; reasons.

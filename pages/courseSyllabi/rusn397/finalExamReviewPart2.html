Dostoevsky beyond Dostoevsky:
Film & Television Adaptations
Inspired by Dostoevsky’s Works

    General Ideas for the Film Adaptations — Key Concepts to Study
  1. What Makes a “Dostoevskian” Film?
    • Doubling
    • Confessional monologues
    • Scandal scenes
    • Crisis of faith vs. rationalism
    • Focus on extreme situations and moral testing
    • Names conveying characters' identity, their function in the novel (for example, Sofia (aka Sonia) is a person touched by divine wisdom (i.e. a holy fool, a hooker with a heart of gold redeeming Raskolnikov)
  2. Key Questions for Studying Adaptations
    • Does the film preserve narrative structure, or does it alter it? How?
    • How does the adaptation translate inner monologue into visual storytelling?
    • Does the film maintain Dostoevsky’s religious and philosophical themes, or shift them toward politics, psychology, or existentialism?
    • How does the protagonist’s moral journey change? Redemption? Irony? Boredom, Despair?
  3. Comparing Novel and Film
    • Be prepared to discuss:
    • • Changes in character motivation
    • • Differences in closure (optimistic/bleak; spiritual/secular)
    • • New cultural contexts (exotic orientalist Russia, 1970s NYC, bureaucratic dystopias)
    • • Narrative tone: serious, serio-comic: dark comedy, satire, noir

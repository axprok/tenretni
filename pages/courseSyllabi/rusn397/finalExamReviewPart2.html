Dostoevsky beyond Dostoevsky:
Film & Television Adaptations
Inspired by Dostoevsky’s Works

    General Ideas for the Film Adaptations — Key Concepts to Study
  • 1. What Makes a “Dostoevskian” Film? • Fragmented narration • Psychological doubling • Confessional monologues • Scandal scenes • Crisis of faith vs. rationalism • Focus on extreme situations and moral testing
  • 2. Key Questions for Studying Adaptations • Does the film preserve narrative structure, or does it alter it? How? • How does the adaptation translate inner monologue into visual storytelling? • Does the film maintain Dostoevsky’s religious and philosophical themes, or shift them toward politics, psychology, or existentialism? • How does the protagonist’s moral journey change? Redemption? Irony? Despair?
  • 3. Comparing Novel and Film Be prepared to discuss: • Changes in character motivation • Differences in closure (optimistic/bleak; spiritual/secular) • New cultural contexts (exotic orientalist Russia, 1970s NYC, bureaucratic dystopias) • Narrative tone: serious, serio-comic: dark comedy, satire, noir

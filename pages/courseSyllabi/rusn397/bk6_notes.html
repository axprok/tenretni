Book 6 recounts the life, teachings, and spiritual experiences of Elder Zosima in a form that deliberately imitates the structure and tone of Orthodox saints’ lives (жития святых). It includes: His conversion story, modeled on the saint’s youthful turning from sin to holiness; His miracles and moral teachings; The testimony of his disciples, who preserve his words; and A deathbed scene filled with reverence and expectation of incorruption. However, Dostoevsky also complicates the hagiographic form — Zosima’s body corrupts after death, and his “life” becomes a spiritual test for others rather than a simple tale of sanctity. So Book VI is both a hagiography and a reflection on the limits of hagiography — a saint’s life reimagined for the modern, doubting age.

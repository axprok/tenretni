Rusn 381. Decolonizing Putin's Russia


The United Nations (UN) states that the fundamental right to self-determination is the core requirement for decolonization, and that this right can be exercised with or without political independence. A UN General Assembly Resolution in 1960 characterised colonial foreign rule as a violation of human rights. In states that have won independence, indigenous people living under settler colonialism continue to make demands for decolonization and self-determination.

    The course introduces students to recent Russian, Ukrainian, Georgian, and Lithuanian cinemas and political culture as its context focusing on
  1. Russia’s imperial legacy (Russian and Soviet),
  2. Cinemas of Decolonization in Ukraine, Lithuania, Estonia, and Georgia
  3. Russia’s current assault against democratic institutions in the region
  4. the place of a film market under authoritarianism and its genres,
  5. the resistance to authoritarian power in arthouse and independent cinemas

Isaac (dir. Jurgis Matulevičius, 2019))

The 2019 Lithuanian film Isaac (Izaokas), directed by Jurgis Matulevičius, explores themes of guilt, memory, and historical trauma, particularly in the context of Lithuania’s Nazi occupation and Soviet rule. The film is based on a short story by Antanas Škėma and revolves around the Kaunas massacre of 1941, when pro-German Lithuanian nationalists killed more than 50 Jewish men at the Lietūkis Garage.

Isaac adapts noir devices to explore Lithuania’s painful history under fascist and Soviet rule. This creates a unique hybrid: a historical-political noir that uses the genre's tools to critique complicity and memory.

For example, later in the film, Andrius returns alone to the site of the Lietūkis garage massacre, the place where he witnessed—or participated in—the killing of Isaac, a Jewish prisoner.

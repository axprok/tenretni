The Russian/Soviet Empire and the Film Authors in its Service

Year Clip from film Significance
1959 400 Blows an example of Art Cinema style (aka Second Cinema) and Francois Truffaut's notion of film auteur
1966 "Andrei Rublev" with Andrei Tarkovskii's voiceover Andrei Tarkovskii: a Soviet film auteur ideologically in opposition to the state, funding still comes from the state
1999 Barber of Siberia a Russian film auteur Nikita Mikhalkov promotes Russian imperial nostalgia. Funding comes from the state. Successful producer of Russian historical blockbusters. Owns his studio TriTe (one of the major Russian studios).
1991 film director Andrei Konchalovsky (brother of Nikita Mikhalkov). An anti-stalinist film. Alert!: Russian political power is still sacralized in the film. Konchalovsky represents Stalin as a magic character (in Svetlana Boym's words: part Satan, part Grandfather Frost from Russian folktales), not a human being answerable for his crimes
2009 Taras Bulba film director Vladimir Bortko made a film erasing Ukrainians as a nation. All Ukrainian cossacks are Russians.
1990s a commercial for Bank Imperial "Tamerlane" Timur Bekmabetov made these commercials the Russian Imperial Bank. His authorial style fuses music video aesthetics with epic narration. A successful Hollywood producer and filmmaker (Ben-Hur, Lincoln: Vampire Hunter, Wanted)

John Buchan (1875-1940)

Buchan graduated from Brasenose College, Oxford. A lieutenant colonel in the Intelligence Corps, the Director of Information—reporting directly to prime minister David Lloyd George—during the First World War, a director at the news agency Reuters after World War I, and the Governor General of Canada.

The novel, The Thirty-Nine Steps (1915), featured Buchan's oft-used hero, Richard Hannay, whose character was partly based on Edmund Ironside, a friend of Buchan from his days in South Africa. A sequel, Greenmantle, came the following year.

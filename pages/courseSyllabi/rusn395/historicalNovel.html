Historical Novel

    features
  • invented by Sir Walter Scott in 1814, the first novel Waverly or Sixty Years Since
  • chance encounter with a stranger (will turn out to be an important historical character later)
  • hero is caught between two fighting sides and recognizes some positive features in both sides. Petr Grinev: “The thought of him was inseparable from the thought of the mercy he had shown to me” (450).
  • use of local color, dialect words (for example, umet is a dialect Russian word for the hotel (Captain's Daughter 405)
  • the action is usually set about 60 years earlier (Captain's Daughter is set in 1775 and written in 1836). It is set during the rule of Catherine the Great
    Books about historical novels
  • Hamnett, Brian. The Historical Novel in Nineteenth-Century Europe: Representations of Reality in History and Fiction. Oxford University Press, 2015.

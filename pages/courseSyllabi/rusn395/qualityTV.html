The Americans (2013-2018).

    Robert Thompson says quality television has the following characteristics (Television's Second Golden Age: From Hill Street Blues to ER, Syracuse University Press, 1997):
  • It must break the established rules of television and be like nothing that has come before.
  • It is produced by people of quality aesthetic ancestry, who have honed their skills in other areas, particularly film. It attracts a quality audience.
  • It succeeds against the odds, after initial struggles.
  • It has large ensemble cast which allows for multiple plot lines.
  • It has memory (flashbacks), referring back to previous episodes and seasons in the development of plot.
  • It defies genre classification (often a fusion genre. In case of The Americans 1 family drama in addition to 2 a spy thriller. 3 remember the nineteen-eighties.
  • It tends to be literary.
  • It contains sharp social and cultural criticisms with cultural references and allusions to popular culture.

The Bond Franchise

    Ian Fleming's Bond variant of the spy novel
  • the glamorous British spy compensates for the lost empire.
  • criminal novel as the machine (Umberto Eco 160), formula (alternation of Tanatos (encounter with a Villain) and Eros episodes (love scenes with a woman in classical novels)) (Eco 156)
  • reactionary in its ideology because it is binary (Eco 162) (racism and misogyny are the side-effects of binarism)
  • borrows from Gothic fiction: the final carnage, preceded by torture and imprisonment (Eco 165). Gothic fiction is characterized by an environment of fear, the threat of supernatural events, and the intrusion of the past upon the present
  • middle brow descrtiptions of the familiar for the mass reader (167)
